abstract: In the realm of information systems handling critical contracts and documents in essential services, the deficiency of protective mechanisms for data subjects is evident. This paper addresses this gap by assessing the utility of named entity recognition in identifying, monitoring, and validating personally identifiable information. Three prominent Natural Language Processing tools (NLTK, Stanford CoreNLP, and spaCy) are employed in our experiments. Initially, the tools' efficacy is evaluated using a generic dataset, followed by their application to datasets derived from contracts containing sensitive information. Results indicate a highly positive performance in accurately classifying both generic and contract data. Additionally, we delve into how our proposed approach serves as an effective Privacy Enhancing Technology for organizations dealing with crucial data subjects in contractual and document-driven domains.
